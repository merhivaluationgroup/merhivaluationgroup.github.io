Damodaran VS Musk

Written by: Oday Merhi

Professor Damodaran describes Tesla as a “story stock”. In his perspective, it is a young company. It was founded in 2003, where in that case it would still be in the stages of lose within the business life cycle. He also sees that it operates in a big market, where a combination of both the automotive industry and semiconductor industry is more than fit to meet that statement. Those two things aside, Professor Damodaran also sees that the company has a great CEO (Elon Musk), who is capable of pushing the boundaries of the company to levels that we may not expect. These three elements put together show Tesla to be a very promising company, where the sky is the limit. That being said, his take on the company’s current price is pessimistic.

 

In his scenarios he finds a wide range of values that yield numbers such as: $69.99, $123.34, and $151.85. His most optimistic scenario yields him values of around $200 to $300, but in those cases he sees that the chances of that happening is very slim. Tesla would need to hit thriving growth rates that are attained from both the automotive and semiconductor industries in order to meet his most optimistic scenario.

 

In our opinion, everything lays on the shoulders of the great Elon Musk. The value of the company will thrive as long as his vision and mission are executed in a way that will allow Tesla to succeed in both industries. Mr. Musk is a great story teller, where he takes us on a journey that is like no other. Damodaran suggests we must revisit the story again and again to see if we arrive at a happy ending.
